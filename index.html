I am not this body

The first lesson of spiritual life begins when we understand that the self is different from the temporary material body.

1. The Linguistic and Analytical Facet/Aspect The most immediate proof that you are not the body is found in how you describe yourself. When you speak of your body parts, you say, "my hand," "my leg," or "my head"; you never say, "I hand" or "I leg". Just as you say, "this is my book" or "this is my shirt," implying you are the owner and distinct from the object, you are the owner or occupier of the body and not the body itself.
